    ADR-001 (`decisions/ADR-001-openscad-include-vs-use.md`) — title "OpenSCAD `include` vs `use` + `$fn` Convention" (D-18). Decision: use `include <params.scad>` for the parameter file (because `use` does NOT export variables → silent `undef` is the failure mode), `use <...>` for furniture/module files (definitions only, no geometry executed at origin), and keep `$fn` low (~12) during development, raised only for committed renders/exports. Consequences must name the `undef` failure mode that this prevents and the render-time tradeoff of high `$fn`.

    ADR-002 (`decisions/ADR-002-dollhouse-print-scale.md`) — title "Doll-House Print Scale = 1:25" (D-19). Decision: lock 1:25 (a Phase-1 success criterion requires this). Rationale to capture: a Bambu Lab A1 (256×256mm bed) fits the whole ~144×168mm 1:25 footprint of a ~3.6×4.2m room and any single ~168mm wall easily; at 1:25 walls are ~3mm — thick enough for the printed joinery in ADR-003; AMS enables printing walls in meaning-bound brand inks (ties to ADR-004). Consequences: exact joint feature-size verification is deferred to a Phase 6 checkpoint.
  </action>

Decision: the doll-house decomposes into a floor/base + individual swappable walls joined by printed connectors, so it can be iterated (reprint one wall to add shelving mounts) — the printed model becomes a living, version-controlled physical object. Decision: brand inks always carry their Signal Path type meaning and are never reused decoratively to mean something else (governs every render, label, and future lighting scene). The Bench repo REFLECTS the Board's color semantics, never INVENTS them (CLAUDE.md Board boundary: identity is read-only from the Board; insights/artifacts flow back as Pulses/Components). Consequences: gaps are marked "pending — owned by the Board"; any proposed new color routes back to the Board, not canonized here; `tokens.yaml` is a pinned snapshot that re-syncs when the Board changes (manual in v1; `make sync-tokens` is a v2 idea).
